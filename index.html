Point Pelee National Park

Point Pelee National Park is a national park in Essex County in southwestern Ontario, Canada where it extends into Lake Erie. The word pelée is French for 'bald'. Point Pelee consists of a peninsula of land, mainly of marsh and woodland habitats, that tapers to a sharp point as it extends into Lake Erie.

History

Aboriginal people lived on Point Pelee for many years before European colonization, dating back to at least 6,000 years.[2] The largest archaeological site found at Point Pelee is thought to have been occupied between AD 700 and 900.

Geography

Located in the western parts of the St. Lawrence Lowlands, the park is a sandspit formation that extends 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) into Lake Erie and is up to 70 metres (230 ft) thick.[10] With an area of only 1,564 hectares (3,860 acres), it is Canada's smallest national park.

Flora and fauna

Owing to its southernly location and the moderating effects of Lake Erie, the climate in the park is slightly warmer than the rest of Canada[10] and many Carolinian faunal species, which are rare in Canada are located here.[3] Examples include the fox snake and the five-lined skink.

Climate

Point Pelee has a humid continental climate (Dfa under the Köppen climate classification) with warm, humid summers, and cold winters that is modified by the surrounding waters of Lake Erie.[3] It lies in a zone that is characterized by variable weather due to conflict between polar and tropical air masses.
